# Funding Memo Prompts

## Purpose

This file contains reusable prompts for turning ecommerce cash flow forecast data into clean, plain-English funding memos, working capital summaries, lender-readiness notes, and internal decision briefs.

These prompts should never imply that the seller is approved, qualified, eligible, or guaranteed for funding.

The goal is to help the seller explain:

- Current cash position
- Forecasted cash low point
- Working capital gap
- Use of funds
- Timing issue
- Inventory need
- Payout delay exposure
- Existing debt obligations
- Repayment pressure
- Documents needed
- Operational fixes already considered

## Required Funding Disclaimer

Use this language in or near any memo:

> This is a forecast-based planning summary. It is not a loan approval, qualification decision, eligibility determination, funding guarantee, or legal, tax, accounting, lending, investment, or financial advice.

## Prompt 1: Create a Funding Memo From Forecast

```text
Using my 90-day ecommerce cash flow forecast, create a funding memo.

Include:
1. Business overview
2. Sales channels
3. Current cash position
4. Forecast period
5. Projected cash low point
6. Minimum safe cash buffer
7. Estimated working capital gap
8. Suggested planning range
9. Main drivers of the gap
10. Planned use of funds
11. Existing debt or financing payments
12. Inventory/restock timing
13. Ad spend considerations
14. Platform payout timing
15. Risks and mitigations
16. Documents needed
17. Disclaimer

Write it in plain English. Do not say I am approved, qualified, eligible, or guaranteed for funding.
```

## Prompt 2: One-Page Working Capital Summary

```text
Turn my forecast into a one-page working capital summary.

Use this structure:
- Current cash
- Forecast period
- Lowest projected cash week
- Cash low point
- Minimum safe cash buffer
- Estimated funding gap
- Suggested working capital planning range
- Primary use of funds
- Why the cash gap exists
- Why the timing matters
- What operational fixes I considered first
- What data still needs to be confirmed

Keep it short, credible, and lender-friendly without sounding like hype.
```

## Prompt 3: Inventory Financing Memo

```text
Create an inventory financing memo based on my Inventory Planner and Cash Flow Forecast.

Include:
1. Inventory purchase amount
2. Supplier/payment timing
3. Lead time
4. Current inventory cover
5. Stockout risk
6. Expected revenue continuity risk if I do not restock
7. Cash after purchase
8. Funding gap if any
9. How funds would be used
10. Repayment risk based on projected cash flow
11. Mitigations such as split order, supplier terms, reduced ad spend, or SKU prioritization

Do not present financing as guaranteed or automatically the right answer.
```

## Prompt 4: Payout Delay Working Capital Memo

```text
Create a working capital memo focused on platform payout delays.

Use my forecast to explain:
1. Which platforms create payout delays
2. Sales made versus cash received
3. Weeks where payouts arrive after expenses are due
4. Cash low point caused by timing
5. Estimated working capital timing gap
6. Suggested planning range
7. What expenses need coverage during the gap
8. What data supports the timing issue
9. What assumptions need confirmation

Use plain English. Do not count gross sales as available cash.
```

## Prompt 5: Funding Readiness Checklist

```text
Using my forecast and uploaded data, create a funding readiness checklist.

Score each item as Ready, Needs Work, or Missing:
- Current cash confirmed
- Revenue trend documented
- Platform payout timing known
- Gross margin / COGS known
- Inventory plan documented
- Use of funds clear
- Existing debt payments listed
- Bank statements available
- Sales reports available
- Ad spend documented
- Tax/accounting issues flagged
- Forecast summary prepared

Then tell me the top 5 cleanup steps before I have a funding conversation.
```

## Prompt 6: Funding Gap Explanation

```text
Explain my funding gap in plain English.

Answer:
1. What is the estimated gap?
2. What week does it appear?
3. What causes it?
4. Is it a timing gap, margin problem, debt problem, spending problem, or inventory problem?
5. What operational fixes should I try first?
6. What would make funding reasonable to consider?
7. What would make funding risky?
8. What data needs to be confirmed?

Be direct and practical.
```

## Prompt 7: Use of Funds Summary

```text
Create a use-of-funds summary based on my forecast.

Break the requested or suggested working capital amount into categories:
- Inventory
- Ads
- Payroll/contractors
- Shipping/fulfillment
- Platform payout timing gap
- Debt/payment timing
- Tax/reserve
- Cash buffer

For each category, explain:
1. Amount
2. Why it matters
3. Week needed
4. What happens if not funded
5. Whether it is essential, useful, or optional
```

## Prompt 10: Internal Go / No-Go Funding Decision

```text
Use my forecast to create an internal Go / No-Go funding decision memo.

Evaluate:
- Is there a real working capital gap?
- Is the gap caused by timing or weak economics?
- Is use of funds clear?
- Can projected cash support repayment?
- Are existing debt payments already too high?
- Are inventory and ad assumptions reasonable?
- Is data quality strong enough?
- What could go wrong?

Give one of these decisions:
- Prepare funding conversation
- Fix operations first
- Gather missing data first
- Avoid new debt for now

Explain why.
```

## Prompt 11: Funding Risk Disclosure

```text
Create a plain-English risk disclosure for my funding memo.

Include:
- Forecast assumptions
- Data limitations
- Repayment risk
- Inventory risk
- Payout delay risk
- Margin risk
- Debt stacking risk
- Why this is not a guarantee or approval

Keep it direct and transparent.
```

## Prompt 12: Working Capital Range Calculator

```text
Calculate a working capital planning range from my forecast.

Use:
- Minimum safe cash buffer
- Lowest projected ending cash
- Inventory purchase needs
- Platform payout delay risk
- Debt payment strain
- Data quality confidence

Show:
1. Funding gap before cushion
2. Suggested cushion %
3. Low planning amount
4. High planning amount
5. Why the cushion is appropriate
6. What could make the range lower
7. What could make the range higher

Do not provide fake precision.
```

## Prompt 13: Funding Conversation Prep

```text
Prepare me for a working capital conversation based on my forecast.

Give me:
1. 30-second explanation of the funding need
2. Plain-English use of funds
3. 5 numbers I need ready
4. 5 documents I should prepare
5. 5 questions I should expect
6. 5 red flags I need to clean up first
7. How to explain repayment without sounding like I am guessing

Keep it practical.
```

## Prompt 14: Funding Memo With Conservative/Base/Optimistic Cases

```text
Create a funding memo that includes Conservative, Base, and Optimistic scenarios.

For each scenario, show:
- Ending cash
- Lowest cash point
- Funding gap
- Main driver
- Confidence level
- Whether funding should be considered

Then summarize which scenario should be used for planning and why.
```

## Prompt 15: Turn Forecast Into Advisor Brief

```text
Turn my forecast into a brief I can send to my accountant, bookkeeper, funding broker, or business advisor.

Include:
- What I am trying to decide
- Key forecast numbers
- Cash low point
- Inventory or ad spend issue
- Debt/payment concerns
- Tax/accounting questions
- Data gaps
- Questions I need help answering

Make it clear, organized, and not dramatic.
```

## Final Funding Memo Rule

The memo should make the seller look prepared, not desperate.

Explain the timing gap clearly, show the numbers, name the risks, and avoid promising outcomes.
