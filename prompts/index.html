Weekly Cash Flow Review

Act as my ecommerce cash flow operator.
Review the spreadsheet data I paste below from my 90-day cash flow planner.
Identify cash risk level, lowest projected cash week, cash low point, drivers of pressure, funding gap, and the top 5 actions I should take this week.
Be direct, practical, and plainspoken. If data is missing, label it clearly.

Before I Spend More Money

Before I spend more money on inventory, ads, software, contractors, or debt payments, review my forecast.
Tell me whether this spend is safe, what it does to ending cash, whether it drops cash below my buffer, whether it creates a funding gap, and what I should cut, delay, split, or negotiate first.
🧪 Scenario Planning

Stress-test decisions before they become expensive confidence.

Use Conservative, Base, and Optimistic cases to test inventory orders, ad spend changes, payout delays, margin compression, revenue drops, and debt stacking.

🛡️

Conservative Case

Lower revenue, delayed payouts, higher costs, more refunds, weaker ads, and no magical rescue money.

⚖️

Base Case

The most reasonable current forecast using normal payout timing, known expenses, and expected inventory commitments.

🚀

Optimistic Case

Upside only when supported by evidence. Hope is not a forecast; it is a beverage choice.

🚪

Decision Gate

Compare yes, wait, reduce by half, revenue down 20%, and payout delayed one week before the next big move.

💸 Funding Memo Prompts

Make the seller look prepared, not desperate.

These prompts turn the forecast into credible funding memos, working-capital summaries, broker intake notes, advisor briefs, and risk disclosures without implying approval or guarantees.

📄

Forecast-to-Funding Memo

Business overview, current cash, cash low point, safe buffer, gap, use of funds, existing obligations, risks, and documents.

📦

Inventory Financing Memo

Explain purchase amount, supplier timing, lead time, stockout risk, cash after purchase, and repayment pressure.

⏳

Payout Delay Memo

Show which platforms create timing gaps between sales made, cash received, and expenses due.

✅

Funding Readiness Checklist

Score current cash, revenue trend, payout timing, margins, inventory plan, debt payments, reports, and forecast summary.

🧮

Working Capital Range

Calculate gap before cushion, suggested cushion, low/high planning range, and data that could change the range.

🧑‍💼

Advisor Brief

Package the forecast for an accountant, bookkeeper, broker, or advisor with clear questions and next steps.

This is a forecast-based planning summary. It is not a loan approval, qualification decision, eligibility determination, funding guarantee, or legal, tax, accounting, lending, investment, or financial advice.
🧭 Prompt chooser

Which prompt should you use?

Pick the situation. Run the correct prompt. Avoid the classic founder move of using a hammer on a spreadsheet fire.

Cash feels tightUse Weekly Cash Flow Review + Cash Risk Dashboard.
Need to decide on inventoryUse Inventory Pressure Review + Inventory Scenario Test.
Want to scale adsUse Ad Spend Review + Ad Spend Scenario Test.
Considering fundingUse Funding Gap Diagnosis + Funding Memo From Forecast.
Data is messyUse Data Quality Review before making any major decision.
Need advisor or broker summaryUse Advisor Brief, Broker Intake Summary, or One-Page Working Capital Summary.
