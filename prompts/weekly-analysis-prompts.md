# Weekly Analysis Prompts

## Purpose

This file contains reusable prompts ecommerce sellers can use with ChatGPT or the Ecommerce Cash Flow Forecaster GPT to analyze their 90-day cash flow planner every week.

These prompts are designed to help sellers:

- Review weekly cash flow risk
- Identify the cash low point
- Spot inventory pressure
- Review ad spend
- Diagnose margin leaks
- Check debt-service strain
- Prepare funding conversations before panic
- Create a practical next-step action plan

## How to Use These Prompts

Every week:

1. Update the spreadsheet with actual numbers.
2. Copy the Dashboard, Cash Flow Forecast, Inventory Planner, Ad Spend Planner, and Funding Trigger Alerts.
3. Paste the relevant data into the GPT.
4. Use one of the prompts below.
5. Ask for a plain-English action plan.

## Prompt 1: Weekly Cash Flow Review

```text
Act as my ecommerce cash flow operator.

Review the spreadsheet data I paste below from my 90-day cash flow planner.

Identify:
1. Current cash risk level: Green, Yellow, Red, or Critical
2. Lowest projected cash week
3. Projected cash low point
4. Main drivers of cash pressure
5. Whether cash drops below my minimum safe buffer
6. Whether a funding gap appears
7. The top 5 actions I should take this week

Separate the analysis into:
- Revenue
- Inventory
- Ad spend
- Operating expenses
- Debt payments
- Taxes
- Platform payout timing
- Data quality

Be direct, practical, and plainspoken. Do not overstate certainty. If data is missing, label it clearly.
```

## Prompt 2: Cash Risk Dashboard

```text
Review my updated 90-day ecommerce cash flow planner and create a Cash Flow Risk Dashboard.

Score each category as Low, Medium, High, or Critical:
- Cash flow risk
- Inventory risk
- Ad spend risk
- Debt-service risk
- Margin risk
- Funding-readiness risk
- Data-quality risk

For each category, explain:
1. Why you gave that score
2. What number or trend caused the score
3. What I should do next
4. What data would improve the score

Use a table and plain English.
```

## Prompt 3: Lowest Cash Week Diagnosis

```text
Find the lowest cash week in my 90-day forecast.

Tell me:
1. Which week cash gets lowest
2. How much cash is projected at the low point
3. Whether that falls below my minimum safe cash buffer
4. What causes the low point
5. Which expenses or timing issues are controllable
6. Which issues are not controllable
7. What I should do in the next 7 days

Do not just summarize the spreadsheet. Diagnose the cash problem.
```

## Prompt 4: Funding Gap Diagnosis

```text
Review my 90-day cash flow forecast and diagnose whether there is a working capital gap.

If there is a gap, calculate:
1. Cash low point
2. Minimum safe cash buffer
3. Funding gap before cushion
4. Suggested planning cushion based on data quality
5. Suggested working capital planning range
6. Week when I should start preparing
7. Main use of funds

Also explain:
- Whether this looks like a timing gap, margin problem, debt problem, or spending problem
- What I should fix before considering funding
- What documents or numbers I should prepare

Do not say I am approved, qualified, eligible, or guaranteed for funding.
```

## Prompt 5: Inventory Pressure Review

```text
Review my Inventory Planner and Cash Flow Forecast together.

Tell me:
1. Which SKUs are at risk of stockout
2. Which SKUs may be overstocked
3. Which planned restocks create cash pressure
4. Whether any purchase order drops cash below my safe buffer
5. Whether I should reorder, delay, split, reduce, or negotiate terms
6. Whether a working capital backup plan may be needed to protect revenue continuity

Explain the tradeoff between stockout risk and cash preservation in plain English.
```

## Prompt 6: Ad Spend Review

```text
Review my Ad Spend Planner against my Cash Flow Forecast.

Tell me:
1. Whether planned ad spend is safe based on projected cash
2. Whether ad spend is growing faster than gross profit
3. Whether any week shows ad spend pressure
4. Which campaigns or channels should be scaled, held, fixed, or cut
5. Whether inventory can support the demand created by ads
6. Whether payout delays create a cash timing issue

Do not treat ROAS as the only metric. Include cash timing, margin, inventory, and runway.
```

## Prompt 7: Debt-Service Stress Test

```text
Review my Debt & Financing tab and Cash Flow Forecast.

Tell me:
1. Total debt/financing payments over the next 90 days
2. Weekly payment burden
3. Debt payments as a percentage of projected net revenue
4. Whether payments create cash strain
5. Whether adding new funding would create payment-stacking risk
6. Which weeks are most vulnerable
7. What I should confirm before accepting any new financing

Use plain English and be conservative.
```

## Prompt 8: Margin Leak Finder

```text
Review my revenue, expense, ad spend, and cash flow data.

Find the biggest cash leaks across:
- COGS
- Platform fees
- Shipping and fulfillment
- Refunds and returns
- Discounts
- Ad spend
- Payroll and contractors
- Software subscriptions
- Debt payments
- Inventory purchases
- Taxes or reserves

Rank each leak by:
1. Estimated cash impact
2. Ease of fixing
3. Urgency
4. Recommended action

Explain where sales are failing to turn into cash.
```

## Prompt 9: Emergency 14-Day Cash Preservation Plan

```text
Using my 90-day forecast, create a 14-day cash preservation plan.

Separate actions into:
1. Today
2. Next 48 hours
3. This week
4. Before the next inventory order
5. Before increasing ad spend
6. Before accepting funding

Include:
- Expenses to pause or delay
- Ads to cut or cap
- Inventory moves
- Supplier conversations
- Debt/payment conversations
- Data I need to confirm
- Funding prep steps if needed

Be practical, direct, and calm.
```

## Prompt 10: Weekly Founder Memo

```text
Turn my updated 90-day cash flow planner into a weekly founder memo.

Use this format:
1. Executive Summary
2. Cash Risk Level
3. Lowest Cash Week
4. Key Cash Drivers
5. Inventory Decisions
6. Ad Spend Decisions
7. Debt / Financing Notes
8. Funding Trigger Alerts
9. Missing Data
10. Next 7-Day Action Plan

Write it in plain English for an ecommerce founder.
```

## Prompt 11: Data Quality Review

```text
Review my spreadsheet data for missing, stale, inconsistent, or risky assumptions.

Identify:
1. Missing critical inputs
2. Numbers that look inconsistent
3. Data that may be stale
4. Areas where the forecast may be overconfident
5. Assumptions that should be labeled
6. Questions I need to answer before making a major cash decision

Give the forecast a confidence level: High, Medium, or Low.
```

## Prompt 12: Weekly Actuals Update

```text
I just updated this week's actuals in my 90-day ecommerce cash flow planner.

Compare actuals versus forecast for:
- Revenue
- Refunds
- COGS
- Ad spend
- Inventory purchases
- Shipping and fulfillment
- Payroll/contractors
- Software
- Debt payments
- Taxes
- Ending cash

Tell me:
1. What changed
2. Whether risk increased or decreased
3. What assumptions need to be updated
4. What I should do differently next week
```

## Prompt 13: Platform Payout Timing Check

```text
Review my sales and payout data.

Tell me:
1. Which platforms create the biggest cash delay
2. Whether gross sales are being counted as cash too early
3. Whether reserves, holds, refunds, or settlement timing affect cash
4. Which weeks show a payout timing gap
5. How I should adjust the forecast

Focus on Shopify, Amazon, TikTok Shop, Etsy, WooCommerce, Stripe, PayPal, and marketplace payouts.
```

## Prompt 14: Before I Spend More Money Prompt

```text
Before I spend more money on inventory, ads, software, contractors, or debt payments, review my forecast.

Tell me:
1. Whether this spend is safe
2. What it does to ending cash
3. Whether it drops cash below my minimum buffer
4. Whether it creates a funding gap
5. What I should cut, delay, split, or negotiate first
6. What decision deadline I should use

Be direct. If the spend is risky, say so plainly.
```

## Prompt 15: Cash Flow Truth Teller

```text
Review my forecast like a cash flow truth teller.

Do not sugarcoat it.

Tell me:
1. What number worries you most
2. What assumption is most dangerous
3. What I am probably underestimating
4. What I should not spend money on yet
5. What I should confirm before making a big decision
6. What my next move should be

Be plainspoken, practical, and founder-friendly.
```

## Weekly Analysis Disclaimer

These prompts support planning and decision-making. They do not create legal, tax, accounting, lending, investment, or financial advice. They also do not determine funding approval, qualification, eligibility, or guarantees.
