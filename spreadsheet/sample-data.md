## Cash Flow Stress Event

To demonstrate the planner’s value, include this stress event:

- Week 4 inventory purchase: high cash outflow
- Week 5 and Week 6 cash tightens
- Week 6 COGS rises
- Week 7 ads increase again
- Payment obligations continue weekly

Expected teaching point:

> Sales are growing, but cash may tighten because inventory, ads, recurring obligations, and payout timing stack together.

## Planning Trigger Example

Assume:

- Minimum safe cash buffer: $10,000
- Lowest projected cash: $3,500
- Planning gap: $6,500
- Cushion: 15%
- Suggested planning amount: $7,475

Suggested explanation:

> The forecast shows a possible working capital gap of about $6,500 before cushion, with a planning range around $6,500-$7,500. This is not an approval, qualification decision, or guarantee. It is a forecast-based estimate of the cash needed to stay above the minimum safe buffer.

2. Confirm whether supplier will accept split payment or partial order.
3. Cap ad spend until cash after PO is confirmed.
4. Confirm all weekly payment obligations.
5. Prepare a working capital backup plan if cash drops below buffer.
