Knowledge Base

Purpose

This file is the operating knowledge source for an assistant that helps ecommerce sellers use The 90-Day Ecommerce Cash Flow Planner.

The planner is designed for sellers using platforms such as Shopify, Amazon, Etsy, TikTok Shop, Walmart Marketplace, WooCommerce, multi-channel ecommerce stores, and marketplace sellers with inventory, ad spend, platform fees, and working capital pressure.

The goal is to help the seller forecast the next 90 days of cash movement, identify avoidable cash crunches, plan inventory purchases, control ad spend, manage debt payments, and recognize when working capital may be needed before the emergency happens.

The tone should be helpful, direct, practical, and slightly urgent. Think: a finance operator helping a founder avoid a preventable cash fire.

Core GPT Behavior Rules

When using this knowledge base, the GPT should:

  1. Explain financial concepts in plain English.
  2. Avoid finance jargon unless it immediately defines the term.
  3. Focus on actionable weekly decisions.
  4. Diagnose cash flow problems by separating revenue problems, margin problems, inventory timing problems, ad spend problems, debt payment problems, tax reserve problems, and operating expense problems.
  5. Never imply that funding is guaranteed.
  6. Never give tax, legal, accounting, investment, or lending approval advice.
  7. Frame funding as a planning conversation, not a magic rescue button.
  8. Push the seller to update actual numbers weekly.
  9. Prefer simple formulas and clear labels over complex finance-model theatrics.
  10. Warn the seller when projected cash turns red, runway falls below target, or inventory orders collide with low cash.

Primary Use Cases

The GPT should be able to help users build the spreadsheet in Google Sheets or Excel, interpret each tab, explain each formula, review a seller’s weekly forecast, identify cash crunch risks, estimate a funding gap, suggest a practical working capital buffer, create weekly cash flow summaries, review inventory restock timing, review ad spend pressure, stress-test debt payments, generate plain-English founder/lender summaries, and convert spreadsheet outputs into action plans.

The 90-day-ecommerce-cash-flow-planner contains these tabs:

The default planner uses a 13-week forecast, which is the practical ecommerce version of a 90-day cash flow view. Thirteen weeks equals 91 days, close enough for operating decisions and easier for founders to maintain weekly.

Tab 1: Dashboard

Purpose

The Dashboard gives the seller a single control panel for the forecast. It contains the main inputs, top KPIs, warning indicators, and funding recommendation.

Manual Input Fields

  • Forecast Start Date: First week of the 90-day forecast. Example: 2026-06-01.
  • Beginning Cash Balance: Cash available at the start of the forecast. Example: $25,000.
  • Minimum Cash Threshold: Lowest acceptable cash balance before alert. Example: $10,000.
  • Target Cash Buffer Days: Desired minimum days of cash runway. Example: 21.
  • Funding Cushion %: Extra buffer added to calculated funding gap. Example: 15%.
  • Tax Reserve %: Percent of net revenue reserved for taxes. Example: 12%.

Dashboard KPI Fields and Suggested Formulas

  • Total 90-Day Net Revenue: =SUM('Cash Flow Forecast'!D3:D15)
  • Total 90-Day Cash Out: =SUM('Cash Flow Forecast'!O3:O15)
  • Net Cash Flow: =SUM('Cash Flow Forecast'!P3:P15)
  • Ending Cash Balance: ='Cash Flow Forecast'!Q15
  • Lowest Ending Cash Balance: =MIN('Cash Flow Forecast'!Q3:Q15)
  • Ending Days Cash Runway: ='Cash Flow Forecast'!T15
  • Funding Gap: =MAX(0,B6-MIN('Cash Flow Forecast'!Q3:Q15))
  • Suggested Funding Amount: =B20*(1+B8)
  • Red / Critical Weeks: =COUNTIF('Cash Flow Forecast'!U3:U15,"*RED*")+COUNTIF('Cash Flow Forecast'!U3:U15,"*CRITICAL*")
  • Next Inventory Purchase Date: =IFERROR(MIN('Inventory Planner'!M3:M20),"None scheduled")
  • Funding Recommendation: =IF(B20>0,"Start funding conversation before lowest-cash week.","No immediate funding gap projected.")

Conditional Formatting Rules

- Lowest Ending Cash Balance below Minimum Cash Threshold: red fill.

- Funding Gap greater than $0: red fill.

- Suggested Funding Amount greater than $0: red fill.

- Red / Critical Weeks greater than 0: red fill.

- Ending Days Cash Runway below Target Cash Buffer Days: yellow fill.

Ecommerce Notes

The Dashboard is not a vanity scoreboard. It is the smoke detector. If Suggested Funding Amount is above $0, the seller should begin preparing immediately. Waiting until cash is gone turns a planning problem into a survival problem.

Tab 2: Revenue Forecast

Purpose

The Revenue Forecast tab estimates weekly sales by platform/channel and calculates net revenue after expected returns and refunds.

Column Structure

A Week #; B Week Start; C Shopify Revenue; D Amazon Revenue; E Etsy Revenue; F TikTok Shop Revenue; G Walmart / Woo Revenue; H Other Revenue; I Gross Revenue; J Returns / Refunds %; K Refunds $; L Net Revenue; M Notes.

Suggested Formulas

Assuming data starts in row 3:

  • I3: =SUM(C3:H3)
  • K3: =I3*J3
  • L3: =I3-K3

Copy formulas down through row 15.

Manual Inputs

Weekly revenue by channel, returns/refunds percentage, and notes about promotions, seasonal spikes, product launches, stockouts, or platform account issues.

Auto-Calculated Fields

Gross Revenue, Refunds $, and Net Revenue.

Ecommerce Notes

Revenue is not cash until it lands and survives refunds. Amazon payouts, Shopify payment holds, PayPal reserves, TikTok Shop settlement timing, and chargebacks can make the bank balance look worse than the sales dashboard. When in doubt, forecast cash conservatively.

Tab 3: Expense Forecast

Purpose

The Expense Forecast tab estimates weekly operating costs including COGS, platform fees, fulfillment, payroll, software, taxes, and other operating expenses.

Column Structure

  1. Week #
  2. Week Start
  3. COGS %
  4. COGS $
  5. Platform Fee %
  6. Platform Fees $
  7. Shipping & Fulfillment
  8. Payroll / Contractors
  9. Software / Subscriptions
  10. Tax Reserve $
  11. Other OpEx
  12. Total Operating Expenses
  13. M Notes

Suggested Formulas

  • D3: ='Revenue Forecast'!L3*C3
  • F3: ='Revenue Forecast'!L3*E3
  • J3: ='Revenue Forecast'!L3*Dashboard!$B$9
  • L3: =SUM(D3,F3:K3)

Copy formulas down through row 15.

Manual Inputs

  • COGS %
  • platform fee %
  • shipping/fulfillment
  • payroll/contractor expenses
  • software/subscription expenses
  • other operating expenses

Auto-Calculated Fields

  • COGS $
  • Platform Fees $
  • Tax Reserve $
  • Total Operating Expenses

Ecommerce Notes

Do not hide product cost, platform fees, refunds, or shipping in vague expenses. Ecommerce cash flow gets murdered in the details. The seller needs to see which expense bucket is creating the squeeze.

Tab 4: Inventory Planner

Purpose

The Inventory Planner helps sellers decide what to reorder, when to reorder, how much cash the purchase will consume, and whether the restock timing creates a funding need.

Column Structure

  1. SKU
  2. Product
  3. Platform
  4. Current Units
  5. Avg Weekly Units Sold
  6. Weeks of Cover
  7. Reorder Point Units
  8. Lead Time Weeks
  9. Target Buffer Weeks
  10. Units to Order
  11. Unit Cost
  12. Planned Order Week
  13. Planned Order Date
  14. Purchase Cost
  15. Restock Alert.

Suggested Formulas

  • F3: =IFERROR(D3/E3,0)
  • J3: =MAX(0,ROUNDUP((E3*(H3+I3))-D3,0))
  • M3: =IFERROR(INDEX('Cash Flow Forecast'!$B$3:$B$15,MATCH(L3,'Cash Flow Forecast'!$A$3:$A$15,0)),"")
  • N3: =J3*K3
  • O3: =IF(F3<=(H3+I3),"RESTOCK NOW",IF(F3<=(H3+I3+2),"PLAN SOON","OK"))

Copy formulas down for all products.

Cash Flow Logic

Inventory purchases should flow into the Cash Flow Forecast using planned order week:

=SUMIF('Inventory Planner'!$L$3:$L$20,A3,'Inventory Planner'!$N$3:$N$20)

Ecommerce Notes

Inventory is often a good problem until it becomes a cash guillotine. A fast-selling product can still bankrupt a seller if restocks hit before payouts, debt payments, or ad spend recovery.

Tab 5: Ad Spend Planner

Purpose

The Ad Spend Planner shows how much cash is being spent on acquisition and whether ad spend is efficient enough to justify the cash drain.

Column Structure

A Week #; B Week Start; C Meta Ads; D Google Ads; E Amazon Ads; F TikTok Ads; G Influencer / Affiliate; H Total Ad Spend; I Revenue Attributed to Ads; J Target ROAS; K Projected ROAS; L Efficiency Status; M Notes.

Suggested Formulas

- H3: =SUM(C3:G3)

- K3: =IFERROR(I3/H3,0)

- L3: =IF(K3>=J3,"Scale / hold","Fix or cut")

Copy formulas down through row 15.

Ecommerce Notes

High ROAS does not always mean safe cash flow. If inventory, payout timing, refunds, debt, and taxes are ignored, even profitable ads can create short-term cash pressure.

Tab 6: Debt & Financing

Purpose

The Debt & Financing tab tracks existing financing obligations, weekly payments, and the 90-day payment burden.

Column Structure

A Financing Source; B Type; C Beginning Balance; D APR / Fee %; E Weekly Payment; F Start Date; G End Date; H Remaining Payments; I Next Payment Due; J Total 90-Day Payments; K Notes.

Suggested Formulas

- H3: =MAX(0,INT((G3-F3)/7)+1)

- J3: =E3*MIN(13,H3)

To calculate weekly debt payments in the Cash Flow Forecast:

=SUMPRODUCT(('Debt & Financing'!$F$3:$F$20<=B3)*('Debt & Financing'!$G$3:$G$20>=B3)*('Debt & Financing'!$E$3:$E$20))

Ecommerce Notes

Debt does not just cost interest. It reduces operating flexibility. A seller can be growing revenue while slowly getting strangled by weekly payments.

Tab 7: Cash Flow Forecast

Purpose

The Cash Flow Forecast is the core operating model. It pulls together revenue, expenses, inventory purchases, ad spend, debt payments, taxes, and cash thresholds to show projected cash health by week.

Column Structure

A Week #; B Week Start; C Beginning Cash; D Net Revenue; E Inventory Purchases; F COGS; G Platform Fees; H Shipping / Fulfillment; I Payroll / Contractors; J Software / Subs; K Ad Spend; L Debt Payments; M Taxes; N Other Expenses; O Total Cash Out; P Net Cash Flow; Q Ending Cash; R Minimum Cash Threshold; S Funding Gap; T Days Cash Runway; U Status; V Notes.

Suggested Formulas

- C3: =Dashboard!$B$5

- C4: =Q3

- D3: ='Revenue Forecast'!L3

- E3: =SUMIF('Inventory Planner'!$L$3:$L$20,A3,'Inventory Planner'!$N$3:$N$20)

- F3: ='Expense Forecast'!D3

- G3: ='Expense Forecast'!F3

- H3: ='Expense Forecast'!G3

- I3: ='Expense Forecast'!H3

- J3: ='Expense Forecast'!I3

- K3: ='Ad Spend Planner'!H3

- L3: =SUMPRODUCT(('Debt & Financing'!$F$3:$F$20<=B3)*('Debt & Financing'!$G$3:$G$20>=B3)*('Debt & Financing'!$E$3:$E$20))

- M3: ='Expense Forecast'!J3

- N3: ='Expense Forecast'!K3

- O3: =SUM(E3:N3)

- P3: =D3-O3

- Q3: =C3+P3

- R3: =Dashboard!$B$6

- S3: =MAX(0,R3-Q3)

- T3: =IF(O3=0,999,Q3/(O3/7))

- U3: =IF(Q3<0,"CRITICAL: CASH NEGATIVE",IF(Q3<R3,"RED: BELOW MIN CASH",IF(T3<Dashboard!$B$7,"YELLOW: RUNWAY LOW",IF(P3<0,"WATCH: CASH BURN","OK"))))

Warning Indicator Definitions

  • OK: Forecast is within guardrails.
  • WATCH: CASH BURN: Net cash flow is negative but not below thresholds.
  • YELLOW: RUNWAY LOW: Days cash runway below target.
  • RED: BELOW MIN CASH: Ending cash below minimum threshold.
  • CRITICAL: CASH NEGATIVE: Forecast goes below zero.

Ecommerce Notes

The Cash Flow Forecast should be reviewed weekly.

The seller should not only ask, “Will I be profitable?” 

They should ask, “Will I have enough cash on the exact week money leaves?”

Tab 8: Funding Trigger Alerts

Purpose

The Funding Trigger Alerts tab converts forecast problems into plain-English decision signals.

Column Structure

  1. Alert ID
  2. Trigger
  3. Where It Checks
  4. Severity Logic
  5. Current Result
  6. Suggested Action
  7. Suggested Funding Amount
  8. Owner / Status

Recommended Alerts

- Cash below minimum threshold: =IF(MIN('Cash Flow Forecast'!Q3:Q15)<Dashboard!$B$6,"TRIGGERED","OK")

- Negative cash projected: =IF(MIN('Cash Flow Forecast'!Q3:Q15)<0,"TRIGGERED","OK")

- Runway below target: =IF(MIN('Cash Flow Forecast'!T3:T15)<Dashboard!$B$7,"TRIGGERED","OK")

- Inventory purchase collides with cash burn: =IF(COUNTIFS('Cash Flow Forecast'!E3:E15,">0",'Cash Flow Forecast'!P3:P15,"<0")>0,"TRIGGERED","OK")

- Ad spend too heavy: =IF(SUM('Ad Spend Planner'!H3:H15)>SUM('Revenue Forecast'!L3:L15)*0.25,"REVIEW","OK")

- Debt payments too heavy: =IF(SUM('Cash Flow Forecast'!L3:L15)>SUM('Cash Flow Forecast'!D3:D15)*0.15,"REVIEW","OK")

- Tax reserve underfunded: =IF(SUM('Cash Flow Forecast'!M3:M15)<SUM('Revenue Forecast'!L3:L15)*Dashboard!$B$9*0.9,"REVIEW","OK")

- Revenue drop risk: =IF(MIN('Revenue Forecast'!L3:L15)<AVERAGE('Revenue Forecast'!L3:L15)*0.7,"REVIEW","OK")

- Funding amount required: =IF(Dashboard!$B$20>0,"ACTION NEEDED","OK")

Notes for Ecommerce Sellers

A funding alert does not mean the seller should automatically borrow money. It means the seller should diagnose the cause and compare options while they still have leverage.

Tab 9: AI Automation Prompts

Weekly Review Prompt

Act as my ecommerce cash flow operator. Review the numbers I paste from my 90-day cash flow planner. Identify the top 5 cash risks, the week they hit, and the decision I need to make this week. Separate revenue, inventory, ad spend, debt, tax, and operating expense issues. Be direct, practical, and prioritize actions that prevent a cash crunch.

Funding Gap Diagnosis Prompt

Analyze the cash flow forecast and explain why the funding gap exists. Separate true working capital need from avoidable overspending. Recommend the minimum funding amount, the suggested funding buffer, and the week I should start the funding conversation. Do not imply approval is guaranteed.

Inventory Restock Prompt

Review my Inventory Planner and Cash Flow Forecast. Tell me which SKUs need restocking, which purchase orders create cash pressure, and whether I should split, delay, finance, or reduce the order. Prioritize avoiding stockouts without starving the business of cash.

Ad Spend Decision Prompt

Compare ad spend, projected ROAS, net revenue, and ending cash. Tell me which campaigns I should scale, hold, fix, or cut without creating a cash crunch. Do not treat ROAS as the only metric; include cash timing and inventory pressure.

Debt Stress Test Prompt

Review my debt payments as a percentage of weekly net revenue and ending cash. Tell me whether taking another loan, advance, or financing product would create dangerous payment stacking. Explain the risk in plain English.

Emergency Cash Preservation Prompt

Create a 14-day cash preservation plan using my forecast. Separate urgent actions, negotiable expenses, inventory moves, ad cuts, debt/payment conversations, tax considerations, and funding preparation steps.

Scenario Planning Prompt

Create three scenarios for the next 90 days: conservative, base case, and aggressive. Explain what changes in revenue, ads, inventory, debt payments, and cash runway under each scenario. Show the weekly decision points.

Founder / Lender Summary Prompt

Turn this forecast into a plain-English founder summary: current cash position, upcoming risks, working capital need, use of funds, payback logic, and the decisions for the next 7 days. Keep it credible and avoid hype.

Platform Channel Risk Prompt

Analyze revenue by channel across Shopify, Amazon, Etsy, TikTok Shop, Walmart Marketplace, WooCommerce, and other sales channels. Identify concentration risk, payout timing risk, refund risk, and platform dependency.

Margin Leak Finder Prompt

Find where cash is leaking across COGS, platform fees, shipping, payroll, software, ads, debt payments, taxes, and inventory purchases. Rank the leaks by cash impact and ease of fixing.

Tab 10: Instructions

The Instructions tab teaches a non-finance seller how to use the planner without needing a finance degree or a CFO robe.

Recommended workflow:

1. Set dashboard inputs.

2. Update weekly revenue by platform.

3. Update expense assumptions.

4. Update inventory status.

5. Update ad spend and ROAS.

6. Update debt and financing payments.

7. Review the Cash Flow Forecast.

8. Review Funding Trigger Alerts.

9. Use ChatGPT prompts for weekly analysis.

10. Repeat every week.

Every week, the seller should replace the prior week’s forecast with actual numbers, revise the next 12 weeks, check cash threshold/risk/runway/inventory/ad/debt pressure, and generate a weekly cash decision memo.

Sample Data Guidance

Example seller: multi-channel ecommerce brand selling apparel, mugs, bundles, and accessories through Shopify, Amazon, Etsy, TikTok Shop, and Walmart/WooCommerce.

Sample assumptions:

- Beginning cash: $25,000

- Minimum cash threshold: $10,000

- Target cash buffer: 21 days

- Funding cushion: 15%

- Tax reserve: 12%

- Weekly net revenue: $25,000 to $40,000

- COGS %: 34%

- Platform fee %: 3.5%

- Ad spend warning: 25% of net revenue

- Debt payment warning: 15% of net revenue

Conditional Formatting Summary

Dashboard: funding gap, suggested funding amount, and red/critical weeks should be red when above zero. Runway below target should be yellow.

Revenue Forecast: net revenue below average by more than 30% should be yellow or red. Refunds above normal should be yellow.

Expense Forecast: COGS %, platform fees, and total expenses above expected should be yellow.

Inventory Planner: RESTOCK NOW should be red; PLAN SOON should be yellow.

Ad Spend Planner: Fix or cut should be yellow.

Debt & Financing: weekly payments above 15% of net revenue should be yellow or red.

Cash Flow Forecast: ending cash below zero should be critical red; ending cash below minimum threshold should be red; runway below target should be yellow; net cash flow negative should be yellow.

Funding Trigger Alerts: TRIGGERED or ACTION NEEDED should be red; REVIEW should be yellow; OK should be green or neutral.

Key Calculations

Beginning Cash Balance first week: =Dashboard!$B$5

Following weeks: Beginning Cash = Prior Week Ending Cash.

Projected Revenue: Net Revenue = Gross Revenue - Refunds.

COGS: =Net Revenue * COGS %.

Platform Fees: =Net Revenue * Platform Fee %.

Inventory Purchases: =SUMIF('Inventory Planner'!$L$3:$L$20,A3,'Inventory Planner'!$N$3:$N$20)

Ad Spend: =SUM(C3:G3)

Debt Payments: =SUMPRODUCT(('Debt & Financing'!$F$3:$F$20<=B3)*('Debt & Financing'!$G$3:$G$20>=B3)*('Debt & Financing'!$E$3:$E$20))

Taxes: ='Revenue Forecast'!L3*Dashboard!$B$9

Net Cash Flow: =D3-O3

Ending Cash Balance: =C3+P3

Funding Gap: =MAX(0,R3-Q3)

Days Cash Runway: =IF(O3=0,999,Q3/(O3/7))

Suggested Funding Amount: Funding Gap * (1 + Funding Cushion %).

GPT Answer Patterns

If the user asks, "Do I need funding?" check projected ending cash, negative cash, runway, inventory purchases, ad spend, debt payments, and whether the need is true working capital or avoidable overspending.

If the user asks, "How much funding should I seek?" use Suggested Funding Amount = Maximum Funding Gap + Cushion, then explain lowest cash week, minimum cash threshold, funding gap, cushion percentage, use of funds, and driver of the need.

If the user asks, "Should I cut ads?" check projected ROAS, cash flow status, ending cash, inventory cover, payout timing, and attribution reliability. Do not say to cut ads simply because cash is low. Cut low-performing campaigns first.

If the user asks, "Should I reorder inventory?" check weeks of cover, lead time, target buffer weeks, planned order week, purchase cost, cash forecast for that week, and funding gap in that week.

If the user asks, "Why am I profitable but broke?" explain that profit and cash are not the same. Common causes include inventory paid before sales convert to cash, ad spend paid before revenue settles, payout delays, returns/refunds, debt payments, tax reserves, owner draws, seasonal stock build, shipping/fulfillment costs, and marketplace holds/reserves.

Compliance and Safety Language

Use this disclaimer when needed: This planner is for cash flow planning and decision support. It is not tax, legal, accounting, investment, or lending approval advice.

Do not say: You are approved. You will qualify. This lender will fund you. This is guaranteed. Ignore your accountant. Do not pay taxes. Take funding no matter what.

Do say: This suggests a working capital conversation may be worth starting. The forecast shows a potential funding gap. Prepare documents early. Compare total cost, payment schedule, and cash flow impact. Stress-test payments before accepting capital.

Short Definition Library

Cash Flow: cash moving in and out of the business.

Net Revenue: revenue after refunds and returns.

COGS: cost of goods sold.

Platform Fees: marketplace, payment processor, or selling platform fees.

Cash Runway: how many days the business can keep operating with available cash.

Funding Gap: the amount needed to keep cash above the minimum threshold.

Suggested Funding Amount: funding gap plus a cushion.

Working Capital: money used to cover short-term operating needs such as inventory, ads, payroll, shipping, and timing gaps.

Inventory Pressure: when the seller needs to buy inventory before enough cash comes back from sales.

Payment Stacking: when multiple loans, advances, or debt payments overlap and create weekly cash pressure.

Final Operating Reminder

The job of this planner is not to make ecommerce sellers feel safe. The job is to make the risk visible early enough to do something about it.

Cash crunches rarely appear out of nowhere. They usually show up in the forecast first.

Do the boring math before the bank account starts screaming.
